I built a website with only one button. Clicking the button takes people to a blog randomly sourced from [this list](https://github.com/mtlynch/hn-popularity-contest-data/blob/master/data/domains-meta.csv). The domain is https://anyblog.cc - feel free to take a spin! 

![Anyblog website screenshot](/anyblog_website.png)

There’s a couple things happened recently that make me want to find more blogs to read.

I have a habit of reading news, analysis, reports, blogs that are mostly from tech industry. Based on [“My Activity”](https://www.mildlyjournaling.com/activity/) page, I read 80-100k words each month. Recently the content I am reading becomes more and more heavily concentrated on AI. It becomes the hottest and biggest topic in the tech industry. It makes me feel a bit overwhelmed - there are so many AI news happening, one can never really catch up with everything.

At the same time, the action of blog writing itself is getting disrupted by AI: it can generate the perfectly customized “blog post” for whatever questions people have. Yes the style and vibe of these generated content are still “machine”-y and does not give one the same feeling of reading human blogs. But from a functional perspective, it is orders of magnitude more efficient than Googling and jumping across links to find what one needs. 

As an example, technical Howtos used to be a big part of tech blogs - people approach various problems, and share their solutions, learnings and experience. I remember Google used to take me to all kinds of blog post (or a StackOverflow answer) when I search for solutions of a problem. Nowadays I rarely need it - 99% of time AI can tell me the exact solution to my specific problem in steps!

To summarize:

1. My current reading contains too much tech and AI
2. Using AI makes me much less likely to discover new blogs
3. A general wonder of what kind of blogs would exist in the AI future

So the website is my way to discover and explore more blogs. 

There are several features I need to add over time. The primary being expanding the candidate pool. The list has 700+ blogs but they are mostly sourced from Hacker News, a tech heavy news aggregation site. I want better diversity. The immediate next step is to find more lists from different sources and have a “submit” button so visitors can add their own.

For now though, I am happy with the website being able to just take me to a random blog. You should try it too, and let me know if any questions!
